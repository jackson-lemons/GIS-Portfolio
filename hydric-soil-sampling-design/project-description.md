# Hydric Soil Sampling Design

**Location:** Yates Mill Park, Raleigh, NC
**Course:** NR 300 – Natural Resource Measurements (NC State University)

## Objective

Design a field sampling strategy to identify potential hydric soils and wetland boundaries along the Steep Hill Creek floodplain.

## Methods

Sampling locations and transects were developed in ArcGIS Pro by integrating multiple environmental datasets:

* NC Wetland Mapping Program (NCWMP)
* USDA NRCS Web Soil Survey (SSURGO)
* Digital Elevation Model (DEM)
* Topographic contours

Transects were placed perpendicular to the wetland boundary and drainage features to capture transitions between upland and potentially hydric soil conditions.

Hydric soil sampling locations were then distributed along these transects to guide field observations.

## Field Data Collection

Field observations are being recorded using ArcGIS Field Maps and include:

Soil color (Munsell chart)

Soil texture by feel

Evidence of hydric soil indicators

Observations of erosion or deposition features

## Tools

ArcGIS Pro

ArcGIS Field Maps

## Status

Ongoing field project. Additional sampling locations and observations will be collected during upcoming field surveys.

## Output

The map associated with this project illustrates the sampling design used to guide field investigation of hydric soils within the study area.
